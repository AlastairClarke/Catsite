The Impact of the Hours Clause on European Windstorm Losses

European windstorm insurance policies commonly have hours clauses but catastrophe models do not commonly account for them. Here is an article I wrote that attempts to quantify the impact of typical hours clauses on annual occurrence losses over Europe.



I used AIR's Extratropical Cyclone model for Europe which has a 10,000-year stochastic catalogue of simulated windstorms. For each storm I had the entry and exit times in every country in the model domain which includes nearly all countries in Europe except the southern states like Portugal, Spain and Italy. I then created hourly-loss profiles for a 12-month windstorm season (Jul-Jun).



To create these profiles I tested two methods: a lazy way called the Uniform method which assumes a country's event loss is spread uniformly over the event's duration in that country; and a clever way called the Downscaled method which maps CRESTA-level losses to hours in the season.



It turns out that the lazy way is good enough for calculating the impact of the hours clause on annual occurrence losses. Typical clauses of say 72-168 hours can increase the occurrence loss by 10-20% across the 2-10 year return period. Insurers that write such policies need to think about fattening this part of the tail, unless they already do so. There are more details in the article.
